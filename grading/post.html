  • Prove that Corona is a Virus

Posted by Aditya Arora on Wednesday, May 20, 2020

Note that the second lemma finishes the problem since \(ONRO'\) are collinear on the perpendicular bisector of \(BC\) and \(AOCO'\) is cyclic with diameter \(OO'\), so \(CORO'NA\) is a virus. Now, we show that \(CORO'NA\) is able to infect the majority of the diagram. We show that after being spread, without social distancing, the convex hull of \(CORO'NA\) can reach at least \(50\%\) of the population \(\triangle{ABC}\). It's simple to check that \(AOCO'\) are the vertices of the convex hull. Now, if \(\theta = \angle ACO\), we obtain $$\frac{[AOCO']}{[ABC]}=\frac{OO'}{d(B,AC)} \geq \frac{d(O,AC)+d(O',AC)}{d(O,AC)+BO}=\frac{\tan(\theta)+\cot(\theta)}{\tan(\theta)+\sec(\theta)}$$ but since $$\left(\tan(\theta)+\cot(\theta)\right)^2 = \tan^2(\theta)+2+\cot^2(\theta) \geq \tan^2(\theta)+1=\sec^2(\theta),$$ we obtain $$\frac{[AOCO']}{[ABC]} = \frac{\tan(\theta)+\cot(\theta)}{\tan(\theta)+\sec(\theta)} \geq \frac{\sec(\theta)}{\sec(\theta)+\sec(\theta)} =\frac{1}{2}$$ as desired. Finally, we show that any part of the population can potentially be reached by some \(CORO'NA\). Note that since \(\triangle{ABC}\) is acute, any point \(T\) in some population has the property that \(\angle ACT\) and \(\angle CAT\) are acute. We show that some \(CORO'NA\) can reach \(T\). Assume, without loss of generality, that \(TA < TC\). let \(TA\) meet the perpendicular bisector of \(AC\) at \(O*\), pick \(O_2\) further away (at least a distance of \(AC\) is sufficient) from \(AC\) on the same side of \(AC\) and on the perpendicular bisector of \(AC\) as \(O*\). Take point \(B\) to be a point on the circle through \(A\) and \(C\) with center \(O_2\) such that \(\triangle{ABC}\) is acute. Then, \(T\) lies on the interior of \(ACO_2\), which is contained within the \(CORO'NA\) of \(\triangle{BAC}\), implying the result. Stay home! \(\blacksquare\)
